Exercise sdj. Let $a_1\le a_2\le a_3\le \dots$ be a sequence of positive integers with $\lim a_{n}/n=0$. Then $a_{k}$ divides $k$ for infinitely many values of $k$. Moreover, each positive integer $d$ is a quotient $d=\frac{k}{a_{k}}$.


Exercise rvw. Let $p\neq2$, $\zeta=e^{2\pi i/p}$ and $\xi=\disp\sum_{j=0}^{p-1}\zeta^{j^2}$. Then $\xi^2=(-1)^{\frac{p-1}{2}}p$.


Exercise yxn. We have $$\det \left(\begin{array}{cccc} a_{0} & a_{1} & \cdots & a_{p-1}\\ a_{p-1} & a_{0} & a_{1} & \cdots\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ a_{1} & \cdots & a_{p-1} & a_{0} \end{array}\right) \equiv a_{0}^{p}+a_{1}^{p}+\dots+a_{p-1}^{p}\mod p$$


Exercise fiz. Let $f(t)$ be a polynomial with integer coefficients. Then $f(e^{2\pi i/p})=0\implies p\mid f(1)$.


Exercise hwd. Let $\zeta=e^{2\pi i/p}$. If $a_0+a_1\zeta+\dots+a_{p-1}\zeta^{p-1}=0$ with $a_0,a_1,\dots,a_{p-1}\in\Q$, then $a_0=a_1=\dots=a_{p-1}$.


Exercise nef. For $\alpha\in\Z/p$ find explicitly $r(\alpha)=\abs{\left\{ (x,y)\in\bra{\Z/p}^{2}:x^{2}+y^{2}=\alpha\right\}}$.


Exercise rju. $x^2=2$ is soluble in $\Z/p$ iff $p\equiv\pm1$ mod $8$.
